Reading the chart
  • The treemap visualization shows the breakdown of the financing budget payoffs level by level.
  • The size of each rectangle represents the outflow of cash and cash equivalents in € Euro (e.g., the public pension scheme rectangle, representing € 12,456.8 million, is approx. twice as large as the Family and youth rectangle, representing € 7,687.1 million.)
    • <>The area covered by the whole treemap is subdivided recursively according to the amount of money they represent, level by level. On the top level, the area is split between Arbeit, Soziales, Gesundheit und Familie; Wirtschaft, Infrastruktur und Umwelt; Bildung, Forschung, Kunst & Kultur; Recht & Sicherheit; Kassa & Zinsen.
  • Items within a sub-category are represented by rectangles that are closely packed together with increasingly larger gaps to the neighboring categories. For example, within the Arbeit, Soziales, Gesundheit und Familie, the area is further subdivided into public pension schemes, family and youth, health care, work, social affairs and consumer protection, and pensions of officials.
  • Items on the bottom level that belong to the same sub-category are visually represented by using the same color.
Interacting with the chart
  • Hover over the rectangles (e.g., family and youth) to get the dedicated value (€ 7,687.1 million) of the sub-category and further information.
Analyzing the chart
  • Seek out the largest rectangular values (or the largest collected group of rectangles).
  • Or seek out the smallest rectangular values (or the smallest collected group of rectangles).
  • Money spent on pensions of officials is approx. equal to the money spent on education.
  • Economy (research) is the sub-category with the least spent million € (€170.4 million ) in comparison to the others in this category (education, science, and research, innovation and technology, art and culture).
