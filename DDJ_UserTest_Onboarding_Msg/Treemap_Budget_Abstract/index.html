Reading the chart
  • The treemap visualization shows the breakdown of hierarchical data level by level.
  • The size of each rectangle represents a quantitative value associated with each element in the hierarchy.
  • The area covered by the whole treemap is subdivided recursively into sub-categories according to their quantitative values, level by level.
  • Items within a sub-category are represented by rectangles that are closely packed together with increasingly larger gaps to the neighboring categories.
  • Items on the bottom level that belong to the same sub-category are visually represented by using the same color.
Interacting with the chart
  • Hover over the rectangles to get the dedicated value of the sub-category and further information.
Analyzing the chart
  • Seek out the largest rectangular values (or the largest collected group of rectangles).
  • Or seek out the smallest rectangular values (or the smallest collected group of rectangles).
